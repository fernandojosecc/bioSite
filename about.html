About Samira

Samira Felix grew up in El Monte, California, a city in the heart of Los Angeles County. From an early age she was surrounded by women who chased their goals — her mother was deeply involved in her elementary school, which gave Samira access to teachers and mentors who consistently encouraged her to pursue higher education.

She discovered journalism almost by accident. It was not until she enrolled in a Journalism 101 class during her first year of college that she found her calling — writing stories that inform people about the world around them so they can form their own opinions and make meaningful decisions.

Her most meaningful work to date is a piece called "Fields of Resistance," which examines how immigration enforcement affects undocumented farmworkers. The story reflects what drives her most — giving a voice to communities whose stories are often overlooked.

She is currently pursuing her first role in the journalism industry, with her sights set on telling stories that matter.
